Our Therapy Approaches

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) is a structured, time-limited psychotherapy that helps individuals identify and change negative thought patterns and behaviors to improve emotional regulation.

Benefits of CBT:

  • Evidence-based approach with proven effectiveness
  • Practical strategies for managing anxiety, depression, and stress
  • Focus on current problems and solutions
  • Typically shows results in 12-20 sessions

Recommended for: Anxiety disorders, depression, panic attacks, phobias, and stress management.

Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT)

Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT) is a cognitive-behavioral treatment that emphasizes balancing acceptance and change by teaching skills in mindfulness, emotion regulation, distress tolerance, and interpersonal effectiveness.

Core Components:

  • Mindfulness: Staying present and fully aware
  • Distress Tolerance: Managing crisis situations effectively
  • Emotion Regulation: Understanding and managing emotions
  • Interpersonal Effectiveness: Communicating needs effectively

Recommended for: Borderline personality disorder, emotional regulation difficulties, self-harm behaviors, and suicidal thoughts.

Psychodynamic Therapy

Psychodynamic therapy is a therapeutic approach that explores unconscious processes and past experiences to understand and resolve current psychological difficulties and improve self-awareness.

Key Features:

  • Exploration of past experiences and their influence on present behavior
  • Focus on unconscious thoughts and feelings
  • Development of self-awareness and insight
  • Examination of patterns in relationships

Recommended for: Depression, anxiety, relationship difficulties, and self-exploration.

Humanistic Therapy

Humanistic therapy is a therapeutic approach that emphasizes personal growth, self-actualization, and the individual's capacity for self-healing by focusing on their present experiences and inherent potential.

Core Principles:

  • Unconditional positive regard for clients
  • Person-centered approach focusing on the individual's experience
  • Emphasis on personal growth and self-actualization
  • Non-directive and empathetic therapeutic relationship

Recommended for: Self-esteem issues, personal growth, finding meaning and purpose, and life transitions.
